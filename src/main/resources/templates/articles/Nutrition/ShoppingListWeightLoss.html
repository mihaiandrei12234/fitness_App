The best healthy grocery list for weight loss includes a wide selection of foods and, contrary to popular belief, many of the best foods for losing weight are not the ‘diet’ foods or ‘low-calorie’ products.

If you are wanting to slim down, boost your energy levels and eat more healthily, our weight loss shopping list is a good one to take with you the next time you head to the supermarket.

Bread and grains

If you are wanting to stay healthy while eating bread and grains, you should look for the following on labels:

  • •Whole grain bread
  • •Whole grain cereal
  • •Oatmeal (rolled oats or steel cut oats)

Meat and fish

The best protein-rich animal food sources are the following:

  • •Lean chicken
  • •Lean turkey
  • •Lean cuts of beef
  • •Tuna steak
  • •Fresh salmon
  • •Pork tenderloin

Packaged and tinned foods

Many packaged foods can contain a lot of empty calories, nasty additives, fats and unnecessary sugars. In order to cut these out, the following options are the best to choose from:

  • •Brown rice
  • •Whole grain pasta
  • •Quinoa, barley and other whole grains
  • •Dried or tinned beans
  • •Dried lentils

Fruits and vegetables

Fresh produce is one of the healthiest choices you can make, with fruits and vegetables providing a wide range of key vitamins and minerals to contribute to good health.

The following options will provide a variety of nutrients:

  • •Dark green leafy vegetables (spinach, kale, chard)
  • •Colourful, spicy vegetables (peppers, chillis, radishes)
  • •Salad vegetables (tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce)
  • •Crunchy vegetables (carrots, celery)
  • •Fresh berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries, blackberries)
  • •Seasonal sweet fruit (melon, pineapple, kiwi)

Dairy

You can get a good amount of calcium and a dose of protein from dairy products and these should be part of any balanced, healthy diet.

Here’s what to look at:

  • •Fat free or semi-skimmed milk
  • •Fat free or low fat Greek yogurt
  • •Low fat cheese
  • •Low fat cottage cheese
  • •Eggs

Foods to avoid

Sauces, while delicious, can be a source of unwanted additives and calories. Cutting back on calories is made much easier by removing high-calorie sauces and spreads from your diet.

Basic seasonings such as salt and pepper or fresh herbs, dried herbs and olive oil are good ways to add more flavour to meals.

You should also avoid processed snack foods, baked goods or high-fat desserts. These are loaded with unwanted additives, preservatives and sugars which will threaten to derail your healthy eating plans.

Conclusion

This healthy shopping list is packed with wholesome foods that are nutritious, with all the key nutrients and vitamins you need to sustain a healthier lifestyle.

You can of course add your own variations to what we’ve listed, but the core of what is above will stand you in good stead when it comes to your food shopping, not to mention meal planning and mindful eating.
